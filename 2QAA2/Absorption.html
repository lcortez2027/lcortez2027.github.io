ABSORPTION

      Meanwhile, we placed four different paper towels — one remains as it is and some were folded in half, folded thrice, and folded four times. These paper towels were put inside the graduated cylinder with 100 mL of water, and we recorded the final amount of water per graduated cylinder after the paper towel was put inside. Based on our observation, the paper towel that was folded into four layers absorbed the most amount of water; this is because layering the paper towel creates additional areas for water to fill. We also noticed that the paper towels can be compared to the small intestine. Villi is present in the inner lining of the small intestine. Like the paper towels which absorb water, villi aid in nutrient absorption through the increase of the surface area for absorption.
